about Seychelles

The Seychelles is an archipelago of 115 islands in the Indian Ocean, off East Africa.

History

Seychelles was uninhabited until the 18th century when Europeans arrived with Indians,
and enslaved Africans. It remained a British colony from 1814 until its independence in 1976.

Population

Seychelles is the smallest country in Africa as well as the least populated sovereign
African country, with an estimated population of 100,600 in 2022.
