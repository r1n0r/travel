Bora Bora is a small South Pacific island northwest of Tahiti in French Polynesia.

History

The island was inhabited by Polynesian settlers around the 3rd century CE.
The first European sighting was made by Jacob Roggeveen in 1722.

Population

Bora Bora's population of roughly 10,000 people live in three villages: Anau, Fa'anui, and Vaitape.
